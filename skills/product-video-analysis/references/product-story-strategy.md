# Product Story Strategy

A product video succeeds when visual decisions grow from product truth rather than from a generic motion-design trend.

## Planning chain

```text
Product truth
→ audience misunderstanding or desire
→ communication objective
→ one creative proposition
→ proof sequence
→ brand reveal
→ emotional close or action
```

## Product truth inventory

Before writing the storyboard, record:

- primary user;
- painful current behavior;
- product mechanism;
- distinctive capability;
- evidence that can be shown visually;
- emotional benefit;
- trust barrier;
- desired next action.

## Choosing the proof form

| Product characteristic | Strong proof form | Suitable motion language | Suitable copy |
|---|---|---|---|
| Faster workflow | Before/after, compressed sequence, rapid causality | hard cuts, directional pushes, short ease-outs | verbs, time saved, fewer steps |
| More control | Cursor-led state changes, layered editing, reversible actions | precise panel slides, anchor transitions, restrained overshoot | “adjust,” “choose,” “keep control” |
| Intelligence | Input → interpretation → useful output | text resolution, clustering, reveal after processing | transformation language, evidence before claim |
| Scale | Pull-back reveal, many examples, system view | camera retreat, grid expansion, cascading items | numbers after concrete examples |
| Personalization | Parallel variants, retained identity, user-specific result | word pivot, morph, controlled variation | “yours,” “for your…” |
| Trust | Clear UI, calm hold, verifiable data | slower ease-out, reduced bounce, stable framing | direct language, fewer superlatives |
| Creativity | Open canvas, surprising combinations, divergent paths | spatial exploration, morphs, layered depth | possibility language, invitations |

## Copy hierarchy

A useful launch-video ladder:

1. **Context** — what changed or what users struggle with.
2. **Tension** — why existing behavior is insufficient.
3. **Proposition** — the new possibility.
4. **Proof** — concrete capabilities or outputs.
5. **Ownership** — who benefits and how it feels.
6. **Reveal** — product name and category.
7. **Close** — consequence, belief, or call to action.

Not every video needs all seven as explicit sentences, but the logic should be recoverable.

## Channel coordination

Avoid making copy, image, motion, and sound all say the identical thing.

- Copy names the idea.
- Image supplies evidence or metaphor.
- Motion shows causality, hierarchy, or emotional character.
- Sound controls attention, weight, and continuity.

Example:

```text
Copy: “more yours”
Image: multiple user contexts
Motion: one retained word pivots into varied cards
Sound: the beat opens into a wider harmonic layer
Result: personalization is stated, demonstrated, and felt without four redundant explanations.
```

## Creative decision test

For every major effect ask:

1. What product truth does it express?
2. What information does it help the viewer understand?
3. What emotion does it create?
4. Could a simpler motion do the same job?
5. Does it preserve the established transition grammar?

If no answer exists, the effect is decoration rather than strategy.
