# Methodology

## Purpose

The method turns a finished product video into a learning system that connects product strategy, narrative, copy, visual design, motion mechanics, sound, and production technique.

## Why the standard is additive

An earlier version of the workflow improved animation naming and timing detail but accidentally removed macro modules such as narrative structure, visual-system analysis, project organization, and exercises. This is the baseline failure the skill is designed to prevent.

The corrected contract is additive:

```text
complete strategic analysis
+ detailed animation analysis
+ sound and copy coordination
+ production guidance
+ learning transfer
= complete report
```

No new specialty section may replace an existing required module.

## Evidence model

The method distinguishes external facts, direct observation, numerical estimation, and creative inference. This makes reverse engineering useful without pretending to reveal hidden source-project settings.

## Sampling philosophy

- Regular interval samples guarantee complete temporal coverage.
- Scene-change samples locate major edits.
- Dense manual samples reveal easing, overshoot, and UI causality.
- Audio windows reveal transients, silence, and tempo candidates.

## Strategic model

The report starts from product truth and communication objective, then explains how copy, visual metaphor, motion, and sound form an argument. Effects are evaluated by their informational and emotional function, not only by aesthetic appeal.

## Learning model

The source film is decomposed into increasingly difficult exercises:

1. static layout reconstruction;
2. one motion event with correct timing;
3. transition continuity;
4. copy and sound synchronization;
5. short sequence reproduction;
6. transfer to a different product.

This prevents learners from attempting the whole film before mastering its dependencies.
